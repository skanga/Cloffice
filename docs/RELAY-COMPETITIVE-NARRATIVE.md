# Relay Competitive Narrative

Date: 2026-03-23
Status: Draft v1

## 1. Competitive Frame
Relay should not position as "better general AI".
Relay should position as "better governed execution for team operations".

The practical comparison is:

- General assistants: broad help, limited workflow control.
- Internal copilots: task-level boosts, weak orchestration consistency.
- Relay + OpenClaw: controlled multi-user execution with policy and audit.

## 2. Relay vs Claude Cowork (Strategic)
Where Claude Cowork is strong:

- Broad horizontal utility.
- Strong assistant UX and ecosystem momentum.
- Fast personal productivity across many task types.

Where Relay can win:

1. Workflow depth for company-specific operations.
2. Explicit policy and approval semantics shaped to business rules.
3. Team-native run ownership, reassignment, and escalation.
4. Stronger enterprise operating model around OpenClaw as execution plane.
5. Productized operational recipes with measurable KPI contracts.

## 3. Relay vs Typical Internal Copilot Programs
Internal copilot pattern:

- Many pilots, fragmented patterns, weak standardization.
- Tool-by-tool AI usage with little shared governance.

Relay pattern:

- One operating interface for multiple workflows.
- Shared policy, shared traceability, shared execution model.
- Faster path from experimentation to repeatable operations.

## 4. Why Architecture Is a Competitive Asset
Relay frontend + OpenClaw backend creates a controllable boundary:

- UX can evolve quickly without rewriting orchestration.
- Orchestration can enforce governance independent of UI.
- Teams can add recipes while preserving safety and observability.

This is harder to replicate with single-surface chat products.

## 5. Wedge Strategy
Initial wedge:

- Finance approvals and exceptions where trust, policy, and audit matter.

Expansion path:

1. Daily operating briefings.
2. Cross-channel feedback synthesis to roadmap decisions.
3. Additional policy-sensitive operational workflows.

## 6. Decision Criteria Used by Buyers
Buyers evaluating AI operations solutions typically ask:

1. Can it act across our systems safely?
2. Can we explain every decision and action?
3. Can multiple roles collaborate on one run?
4. Can we enforce approvals for high-risk steps?
5. Can we show measurable operational improvement?

Relay should answer yes to all five in product behavior, not just messaging.

## 7. Objection Handling
Objection: "We already have Claude/Copilot."
Response:

- Keep those for broad individual work.
- Use Relay where team-level execution, governance, and traceability are required.

Objection: "We can build this in-house."
Response:

- Most in-house efforts stall at pilot scale due to governance and operating complexity.
- Relay provides a ready control plane pattern with faster time-to-value.

Objection: "AI is still unreliable."
Response:

- Relay is built around plan visibility, explicit approvals, policy checks, and audit receipts.
- Human judgment remains in control for consequential actions.

## 8. Positioning Statement
For operations teams who need AI to do real work, Relay is the governed execution interface that turns AI reasoning into controlled outcomes across systems. Unlike generic cowork assistants, Relay is built for policy-aware multi-user workflows on top of OpenClaw, with explicit approvals and complete traceability.
